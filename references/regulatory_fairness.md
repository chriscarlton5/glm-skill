# Regulatory and Fairness Screening

This skill does not provide legal or regulatory advice. Document assumptions and recommend jurisdiction-specific review when restrictions may affect variable eligibility, rating plans, filings, or consumer impact.

## Screening Checklist

For each candidate variable, record:

- availability at quote/rating time
- business purpose and actuarial rationale
- direct protected-class status
- plausible proxy risk
- jurisdiction or line-specific restrictions
- consumer explainability
- data quality and refresh stability
- scoring availability and missing/new-level fallback
- adverse impact or distributional concern
- filing support and monitoring plan

## Common Sensitive or Restricted Areas

Review state, line, and company policy before using variables related to credit, education, occupation, income, gender, marital status, age, geography, telematics, prior insurance, lapse, non-pay cancel, and any variable correlated with protected classes.

## Documentation Requirement

The final report should distinguish:

- modeled indication
- selected actuarial judgment
- business constraint
- regulatory or fairness constraint
- manual override

Do not claim a variable is legally allowed unless the user provided that determination or a qualified source is available.
